Creatives · Context
What is a Marketing Message (MM)?

A promotional message sent directly to a customer's WhatsApp inbox, not a feed, not a banner.

When the customer replies, it opens a real two-way conversation between them and the business.

Product launches Promotions Re-engagement Direct-response
My Role
Lead Designer · Marketing Message Experience team

Creatives · Context
MM lives in Ads Manager, but it's not like other ads
Opted-in subscribers only, for re-engagement

Not broad audience acquisition like standard ads. MM reaches people who've already signed up to hear from that business, closer to email marketing than display ads.

Set up inside Ads Manager

Despite these differences, MM is created with the same budget and workflow advertisers already use.

But MM delivery rate was near zero. Most advertisers never set it up at all.
Research
Why aren't advertisers setting up MM?
Creatives · Phase 1 · Problem
MM was buried in Ad Creative, where it didn't belong

MM setup was hidden inside the Ad Creative card. Advertisers had to find an "Edit marketing message" button to open a separate modal, disconnected from the main workflow.

→ Most advertisers published without ever setting up MM. Delivery rate was near zero
→ The modal felt like a completely different product
"Advertisers think of ads and MM as one workflow, the structure didn't match that."
"Edit marketing message" opened a separate modal
Creatives · Phase 1 · User Research
Advertisers don't think of ads and MM as two separate tasks

User interviews revealed a consistent pattern: advertisers expected MM to be part of the ad setup, not hidden somewhere else. Many had never found the option at all.

"Where is the marketing message setup? I can't find it."
"I didn't even know there was a marketing message option."
Creatives · Phase 1 · Restructure
MM moved out of a modal and into Ad Creative

MM had lived inside a buried section of Creative, disconnected from the ad setup advertisers already knew. The restructure moved it to the same level, making the connection obvious.

Creatives · Phase 1 · Design
MM text field pre-fills from the ad, never blank

MM's primary text is a required field. If it's blank, the ad can't go live. That's the direct connection to delivery rate: pre-filling it from the ad copy meant MM would always launch with a valid message, even if the advertiser never touched it.

→ Removes the blank-page problem
→ Advertisers who customize can; those who don't have a working default
MM text field, pre-filled, labeled, distinct from ad copy
Creatives · Phase 1 · Gen AI
Gen AI turned customization into a one-tap decision

Gen AI text generation was introduced natively within the creative wizard. The MM field surfaces performance-optimized alternatives so advertisers can accept a suggestion or refine the pre-filled text manually, without ever leaving Ads Manager.

Single suggestion, accept and replace, or ignore

Creatives · Phase 2 · Decision
Went with Direction 2 as the MVP

To validate the hypothesis, we chose Direction 2. Faster to ship, lower eng lift, and enough signal to know if it worked. With room to go further if it did.

Direction 1: Unified text, set aside for now
Required Gen AI model change and XFN alignment. Stronger long-term vision, but not the right scope to start.
Research
With Direction 2 chosen, how visible should the customization option be?
Creatives · Phase 2 · Exploration
Two directions, one question: how visible should MM be?

I explored two directions to find the right balance between MM visibility and UI simplicity. I ran an A/B test to decide.

Direction 1 · Unified ad & MM text
Direction 2 · Conditional MM customization · Chosen
Creatives · Phase 2 · A/B Test
Option A: Progressive disclosure

Displays options only when needed. By default, uses ad multi-text options to keep the UI clear and focused while allowing users to explore more choices when needed.

+Keeps the UI simple and focused
+Reduces visual noise and cognitive load
−MM customization may be missed unless advertisers expand it
Creatives · Phase 2 · A/B Test
Option B: Full visibility

Displays all options at once for immediate comparison. Makes MM customization more visible and easier to discover, encouraging higher engagement.

+Provides greater visibility and prominence for MM
+Advertisers can compare and understand options at a glance
+Reduces the need for additional clicks or exploration
−UI may appear visually dense or cluttered
−Increased cognitive load when multiple elements compete for attention
Creatives · Phase 2 · Results
Full visibility wins, 16% more advertisers customized

10% → 50% → 100% global rollout.

Creatives · Phase 3 · North Star
What if we didn't need two separate text inputs at all?

The A/B results confirmed the hypothesis: reducing friction changes behavior. That raised the question: what if the friction wasn't in the UI, but in having two separate inputs at all? When the ads team started a creative setup redesign, partnering in early opened the door to push further.

01
One unified text field

Single input for both ads and MM. No separate section to find.

02
Gen AI differentiates the output

Same source text → tailored suggestions for ads vs. MM separately.

03
Simpler at every level

Less UI complexity, less cognitive load, smaller engineering surface area.

Conversations · Solution
Chat Builder, a new first-class surface in Ads Manager

Auto Reply and Quick Replies moved into their own dedicated card, Chat Builder, at the same level as Creative. A 0-to-1 product surface that had never existed before in Ads Manager.

Rather than inventing a new pattern, the design followed CTWA's established L1 structure, so advertisers wouldn't need to relearn the mental model across product surfaces.

Chat Builder, new first-class section at L1
Conversations · Solution · Design Details
Two design decisions that made Chat Builder work
Auto-saves on publish

Setup saves automatically when the campaign is published. Second campaign takes seconds. Already configured, nothing to redo.

Live preview as you type

The right panel shows exactly what the consumer sees, updating in real time. No publishing blind.
