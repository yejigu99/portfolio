Creatives · Context
What is a Marketing Message (MM)?

A promotional message sent directly to a customer's WhatsApp inbox, not a feed, not a banner.

When the customer replies, it opens a real two-way conversation between them and the business.

Product launches Promotions Re-engagement Direct-response
My Role
Lead Designer · Marketing Message Experience team
Set design direction across a team of 3 designers · Owned creatives & conversations end-to-end
Creatives · Context
MM lives in Ads Manager, but it's not like other ads
Opted-in subscribers only, for re-engagement

Not broad audience acquisition like standard ads. MM reaches people who've already signed up to hear from that business, closer to email marketing than display ads.

Set up inside Ads Manager

Despite these differences, MM is created with the same budget and workflow advertisers already use.

Creatives · Phase 1 · Problem
MM was buried in Ad Creative, where it didn't belong

MM setup was hidden inside the Ad Creative card. Advertisers had to find an "Edit marketing message" button to open a separate modal, disconnected from the main workflow.

→ Most advertisers published without ever setting up MM
→ The modal felt like a completely different product
"Advertisers think of ads and MM as one workflow, the structure didn't match that."
"Edit marketing message" opened a separate modal
Creatives · Phase 1 · Solution
IA restructure, moved post-publish tools out of Creative

Auto Reply and Quick Replies only activate after a consumer replies, they don't belong in Creative setup. Moving them out unlocked a cleaner structure for both surfaces.

Creatives · Phase 1 · Design
MM text field pre-fills from the ad, never blank

MM is brand new to most advertisers. A blank field creates friction, so the MM text field pre-fills from the primary ad copy as a starting point.

→ Removes the blank-page problem
→ Still clearly labeled as a separate WhatsApp message
→ Advertisers who want to customize can, those who don't have a working default
MM text field, pre-filled, labeled, distinct from ad copy
Creatives · Phase 1 · Gen AI
Gen AI v1 shipped, but customization stayed at 1.4%
Single suggestion, accept and replace, or ignore

Gen AI surfaced one MM suggestion inline in the wizard. Accept it to replace your text, or ignore it. Simple and low-risk for v1.

The pivot

MM customization rate: 1.4%. The all-or-nothing format wasn't the problem — most advertisers never even saw the option. That reframe became my brief for Phase 2.

Creatives · Phase 2
Ad text variants could power MM — without extra work

Phase 1 told me the problem was visibility, not content quality. So I looked at what advertisers were already doing — and found they were already writing multiple ad text variants. The insight: instead of asking them to write new MM text from scratch, let those variants power MM automatically.

Creatives · Phase 3 · North Star
What if we didn't need two separate text inputs at all?

The A/B results confirmed my hypothesis — reducing friction changes behavior. That made me ask: what if the friction wasn't in the UI, but in having two separate inputs at all? When the ads team started a creative setup redesign, I partnered in early and used that opening to push the question further.

01
One unified text field

Single input for both ads and MM. No separate section to find.

02
Gen AI differentiates the output

Same source text → tailored suggestions for ads vs. MM separately.

03
Simpler at every level

Less UI complexity, less cognitive load, smaller engineering surface area.

Roadmap influenceI pitched the unified direction to PM, engineering, and content. They accepted it — this set the Phase 3 roadmap direction.

Conversations · Solution
Chat Builder, a new first-class surface in Ads Manager

I moved Auto Reply and Quick Replies into their own dedicated card, Chat Builder, at the same level as Creative. A new 0-to-1 product surface that had never existed before in Ads Manager.

Followed CTWA's established pattern

Rather than inventing a new pattern, I looked at what already existed in Ads Manager. CTWA had this structure at L1 — I proposed the same for MM so advertisers wouldn't need to relearn the mental model.

Auto-saves on publish

Live preview as you type
